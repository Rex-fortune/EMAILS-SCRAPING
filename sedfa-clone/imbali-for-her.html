Imbali For Her

Overview

Imbali for Her is a flagship national programme designed to promote inclusive economic participation among women by providing integrated financial and non-financial support.

Built from the voices of women across South Africa, it responds directly to the challenges women face in starting and growing sustainable businesses.


Our Focus

All categories of formally registered women-owned enterprises — from early-stage startups to established small businesses.


Our Goal

To drive inclusive growth by equipping women with the tools, support, and opportunities they need to succeed.


What We Offer
  • Access to funding - blended finance (loans and grants)
  • Business training and skills development
  • Mentorship and networking opportunities
  • Safe, supportive spaces for women in business
Target Audience
  • Women entrepreneurs at all stages of enterprise development, from startup to established small businesses.
  • Women entrepreneurs operating across all economic sectors within the Republic of South Africa
  • Women entrepreneurs in urban, peri-urban, township, and rural areas
  • Women entrepreneurs with disabilities

Funding
  • Up to R5 million (depending on enterprise stage and viability assessment)

Key Requirements
  • Minimum 51% black female-owned
  • South African citizen
  • Registered, tax-compliant business

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)



What is Imbali For Her and how did the programme come about?

Imbali For Her is a women entrepreneurship programme of the Small Enterprise Development and Finance Agency. It is the first fully integrated SEDFA support initiative that combines financial assistance with non-financial support interventions such as business development training, mentorship, and market access support.

The programme emanates from government’s commitment to strengthen women’s economic participation and expand support for women-owned enterprises across South Africa.

What is the meaning behind the name Imbali For Her?

“Mbali” means flower, symbolising growth, resilience, and potential.

“For Her” reflects the programme’s dedicated focus on empowering women entrepreneurs.

Together, the name represents the nurturing and advancement of women-owned enterprises.

How did women entrepreneurs contribute to the design of the programme?

The design of Imbali For Her was directly informed by grassroots consultations with more than 4,500 women entrepreneurs across all nine provinces.

These engagements included:

  • in-person provincial dialogues
  • a national online survey

The consultations confirmed that access to finance alone is insufficient. Women entrepreneurs consistently highlighted the need for a holistic support ecosystem that includes:

  • access to capital
  • mentorship
  • skills development
  • psychosocial support
  • market access opportunities

The programme was therefore designed to respond directly to these identified needs.

What makes Imbali For Her different from other support programmes?

Mbali For Her is a single, unified programme open to all eligible women entrepreneurs. The programme supports enterprises across all stages of business development, from startups and early-stage enterprises to established businesses seeking expansion and growth support.

The programme also includes enhanced provisions aimed at improving access for:

  • Women with disabilities
  • Rural women entrepreneurs
  • Township-based enterprises
What are the qualifying criteria?

Key qualifying criteria include:

  • The enterprise must be at least 51% owned by black women. The woman owner must hold a key managerial position and be actively responsible for:
    • Day-to-day operations
    • Financial oversight
    • Strategic decision-making
  • Applicants must operate a formally registered business. The enterprise must be legally constituted and registered with the Companies and Intellectual Property Commission.
  • The business must comply with all relevant South African legislation and regulatory requirements, including:
    • Tax compliance with South African Revenue Service
    • BBBEE requirements
    • UIF obligations
    • COIDA requirements
  • The programme is open to enterprises operating across all economic sectors. However, enterprises in high-impact sectors with demonstrated employment creation potential and community benefit may receive additional weighting during the assessment process.
How will women entrepreneurs benefit from the programme?
  • The programme offers both financial and non-financial support
  • Financial Support with blended finance model combining grants and concessional loans
  • Funding of up to R5 million, depending on the stage of the enterprise and viability assessment
  • Interest rates at Prime minus 3% per annum
  • Prime minus 5% per annum for women with disabilities
  • Non-Financial Support with tailored support interventions may include:

Important Note:

*Women entrepreneurs approved under Imbali For Her may not simultaneously receive funding from other SEDFA direct lending programmes.

What is the total investment in the programme?

The programme represents a significant investment in women entrepreneurship development over the next three years:

  • R645 million allocated for financial support (loans and grants)
  • R210 million allocated for non-financial support interventions, including, business development support, training, and mentorship
  • R42 million allocated for programme administration
  • This brings the total programme investment to approximately R897 million
How can women entrepreneurs apply?
  • Applications can be submitted online through www.sedfa.org.za
  • Women entrepreneurs who require assistance may visit their nearest SEDFA branch office. Branch contact details are available on the website
