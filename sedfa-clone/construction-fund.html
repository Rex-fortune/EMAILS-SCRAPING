Construction Fund

Overview

The Construction Fund is a joint initiative aimed at strengthening access to finance for small medium enterprises within the construction sector.

  • SEDFA will provide financial products and services to support enterprise growth.
  • CIDB will ensure regulatory compliance and sector development

To roll out the Fund, SEDFA and CIDB will jointly issue a Request for Information (RFI) inviting proposals from qualified non-banking financial institutions (intermediaries).


Expected Role of Selected Intermediaries

Successful applicants will be required to extend lending and credit facilities to MSMEs across the construction value chain.

Successful applicants will be required to extend lending and credit facilities to MSMEs across the construction value chain.

  • Roads and civil construction
  • Built environment (residential, industrial, and commercial)
  • Production and supply of construction materials
  • Electrical installations, energy infrastructure, and related services

Compliance and Development Requirements

Intermediaries must ensure that funded enterprises:

  • Comply with all applicable construction regulations.
  • Are registered with CIDB.
  • Contribute to local employment by employing South African citizens.
Eligibility Criteria

The proposals must meet the following criteria for RFIs:

  • The applicant must be a juristic person legally constituted with a fixed physical address, as defined in the Companies Act.
  • Preference will be given to RFIs to > or = to 51% black ownership.
  • The project must demonstrate ability to generate the level of developmental impact as stated in the Sedfa Corporate Plan such as job maintenance and/or creation and economic empowerment.
  • Financed operations must be conducted within the boarders of South Africa.
  • Applicant must be a non-banking financial institution (intermediary) and demonstrate the capability and capacity to provide credit lending instruments to MSMEs.
  • The applicant must be registered as a credit provider by the NCR.
  • Track record in financing construction MSMEs and related projects or construction order book.
  • Demonstrate ability in assisting MSMEs in negotiating discounts with material suppliers.
  • Ensure MSME’s to be funded are compliant with all applicable laws governing the construction sector.
  • All funded MSME’s must employ South African Citizens only.
  • Ensure MSME’s to be funded are CIDB registered.
  • Must be able to collect and report meaningful operational trends of the MSME’s which may lead to informed decisions on MSME’s in the construction sectors interventions.
  • Proof of 20% match funding for value applied for or an existing lending book.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)



What is the Construction Fund?

The Construction Fund is a joint initiative between SEDFA and the Construction Industry Development Board (CIDB) aimed at developing the construction sector and improving access to finance for small and medium enterprises (SMEs) operating in key priority sub-sectors of the construction industry. 

The Fund will focus on supporting SMEs operating in: 

  • Roads and civil construction.
  • Residential, industrial, and commercial construction.
  • Construction materials supply
  • Electrical and energy-related infrastructure
  • Plumbing
What are the roles of SEDFA and CIDB?
  • The Construction Fund is a R300 million initiative jointly funded by SEDFA and the Construction Industry Development Board (CIDB), with both institutions contributing equally toward the establishment and implementation of the Fund. 
  • SEDFA provides financial products and services to support SME growth.  
  • The Construction Industry Development Board (CIDB) ensures regulatory compliance and supports sector development.  
How is the Fund going to be implemented 

SEDFA and the Construction Industry Development Board (CIDB) will source implementation support through SEDFA’s wholesale funding model, which utilises non-banking financial institutions and other intermediaries to deliver financial services to qualifying enterprises.  Following the official launch of the programme, an online portal will be opened for intermediaries (non-banking financial institutions and other intermediaries) to apply to participate in the implementation of the Construction Fund. 

Which non-banking financial institutions are eligible to apply?

Non-banking financial institutions that: 

  • Operate within South Africa (preference for ≥51% Black-owned entities)
  • Demonstrate a proven track record in financing construction MSMEs or managing a credible construction order book
  • Be registered with the National Credit Regulator (NCR)
  • Have the capacity to design and deploy suitable credit-lending instruments for SMMEs
  • Have systems and infrastructure for MSME onboarding, training, and support
  • Demonstrate ability to collect and report on MSME performance data
  • Provide proof of 20% match funding or an existing lending book
What are the timelines for the RFI submissions? 

Applications open on 13 May 2026 and close on 24 June 2026. Submissions can be done through the SEDFA website.  

What can applicants expect after their submissions? 

SEDFA will assess applications against the approved eligibility criteria. Financial institutions with the requisite capacity, operational reach, and systems capability will be onboarded as intermediaries to deliver the Construction Fund in line with the partnership framework between SEDFA and the Construction Industry Development Board (CIDB). 

What are the expectations once the intermediaries are on board? 

These intermediaries will be responsible for administering the blended finance program, facilitating access to funding, and providing ongoing support to qualifying small construction enterprises in accordance with the objectives and implementation framework of the Fund. 

How can institutions apply? 

Eligible financial institutions can submit applications online at: 

  • www.sedfa.org.za
What is the expected impact of the Construction Fund? 

The Fund aims to:

  • Improve access to finance for construction SMEs.
  • Strengthen participation across the construction value chain.
  • Support job creation and local economic development.
  • Enhance sector transformation and sustainability.
What is the expected impact of the Construction Fund? 

The provision of credit through retail intermediaries is backed by traceable, auditable and enabling technology, meaningful business training and potential bulk buying opportunities that will propel the entrepreneur to exploit economies of scale thereby creating an easier path to sustainability.  

Some of the benefits of using intermediaries include: 

  • Easier access to ready cohort of construction MSMES that are already in the pipeline and on the books of the RFIs and Fund Managers.
  • Easier collection of loans by the intermediaries as they already have existing and performing loan books that include construction small businesses.
  • Easier path to sustainability as the intermediaries offer more than just loans to the enterprises. They also provide technical and business training to assist the MSMEs.
  • Ready order book for the MSMEs, as some of the intermediaries are part of the construction value chain and already have a significant construction order book.
  • Leveraging on existing intermediaries' technology, including robust loan management systems.  
  • The partners already have access to a network of suppliers including stock and input supplies for the entrepreneurs.
  • Continued funding access for the entrepreneurs as they will continue to be clients on the books of the intermediaries.
  • Easy reporting and tracking for SEDFA and CIDB, as the activities of the entrepreneurs are done through the central systems of the intermediaries and fund managers. 
  • Easy impact measurement for SEDFA and CIDB, as the intermediaries have existing systems, measurement and reporting tools.
